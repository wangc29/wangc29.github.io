I finished my PhD in statistical genetics in 2023 and moved into industry shortly after. Looking back, there are a few things I wish someone had told me before I made the switch.

## The skills that transferred immediately

Statistical rigor, comfort with messy data, and the ability to read and critique methods papers. These matter just as much in pharma as in academia — maybe more, because the stakes are higher and the timelines are shorter.

## The skills I had to build quickly

**Communicating to non-statisticians.** In academia, your audience knows the jargon. In industry, you're often presenting to clinical leaders, program managers, or executives who need the bottom line. Learning to translate technical work into clear decisions was the biggest adjustment I made.

Also: **SQL**. Learn SQL. You will use it every single day.
